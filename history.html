Muscat Governorate (محافظة مسقط)

Famous Forts of Muscat (Al Jalali & Al Mirani)

Al Jalali and Al Mirani are ancient forts located in the harbor of Old Muscat. Built by the Portuguese in the 16th century, these forts stood as strong defensive shields protecting the city. Today, they represent great military architecture and offer a spectacular view of the Sea of Oman.

Mutrah Souq & Corniche (سوق مطرح وكورنيش مطرح)

Mutrah Souq is one of the oldest marketplaces in the Arab world. Located adjacent to the vibrant harbor, it features narrow winding alleys filled with the rich aroma of frankincense, Omani silver, and local handicrafts. Right next to it is the Mutrah Corniche, a spectacular waterfront promenade surrounded by dramatic mountains, perfect for peaceful evening strolls.

Bait Al Zubair Museum (بيت الزبير)

Bait Al Zubair is a private cultural heritage museum in old Muscat. It houses an extensive collection of ancient Omani weaponry, traditional costumes, daggers (Khanjars), and historic jewelry that beautifully display the authentic cultural lifestyle of Oman.

Al Alam Palace (قصر العلم العامر)

Al Alam Palace is the elegant ceremonial palace of His Majesty the Sultan. Celebrated for its eye-catching gold and blue pillars, the palace stands as a magnificent monument of modern Islamic architecture located right between the historic forts.

Al Dakhiliyah Governorate (محافظة الداخلية)

Nizwa Fort (قلعة نزوى)

Nizwa Fort is one of the oldest and most famous castles in Oman, built in the 17th century by Imam Sultan bin Saif Al Yarubi. It is unique for its massive circular tower, which is the largest in the Arabian Peninsula. It reflects the deep history of Nizwa when it was the capital of Oman.

👉 Book this destination in one click

Jabal Akhdar - The Green Mountain (الجبل الأخضر)

Jabal Akhdar is part of the Al Hajar Mountain range. It is famous for its unique Mediterranean climate, agricultural terraces, and stunning landscapes. It is world-renowned for producing pomegranates, walnuts, apricots, and the famous Omani rose water.

👉 Book this destination in one click

Al Hoota Cave (كهف الهوته)

Estimated to be over 2 million years old, Al Hoota Cave is located at the foot of Jabal Shams. It is one of Oman’s top natural attractions, famous for its incredible stalactites and stalagmites, as well as a large underground lake that is home to rare blind cave fish.

South Sharqiyah Governorate (محافظة جنوب الشرقية)

Ras Al Jinz Turtle Reserve (محمية رأس الجنز)

Ras Al Jinz is a world-renowned ecotourism destination located in Sur. It is a natural sanctuary for the endangered Green Turtles, where thousands of turtles migrate annually to lay their eggs on the pristine beaches, offering an unforgettable wildlife experience.

Sunaysilah Fort in Sur (حصن السنيسلة)

Sunaysilah Fort is a classic 300-year-old Omani castle built on a high hill overlooking the historic coastal city of Sur. It was constructed to protect the city and its traditional maritime trade routes. The fort features four circular watchtowers and traditional defense structures.

Al Buraimi Governorate (محافظة البريمي)

Al Khandaq Fort (حصن الخندق)

Al Khandaq Fort is one of the most important historical landmarks in Al Buraimi. Named after the deep trench (Khandaq) that surrounds it for defense, this traditional square fort reflects ancient Omani military engineering and served as a central governance hub for centuries.

Al Hillah Fort (حصن الحلة)

Located in the heart of Al Buraimi oasis, Al Hillah Fort is famous for its unique Islamic plaster decorations, elegant rooms, and high protective walls. It stands as a beautiful symbol of Omani social life and governance architecture in the region during ancient times.

Dhofar Governorate (محافظة ظفار)

The Land of Frankincense Sites

Dhofar is the historical capital of Frankincense. It includes UNESCO World Heritage sites like the ancient ports of Al Baleed and Samharam, and the natural reserve of Wadi Dawkah. These sites linked Oman to ancient empires through the famous historic trade routes.

👉 Book this destination in one click

Wadi Darbat (وادي دربات)

Wadi Darbat is a breathtaking natural park in Dhofar's mountains. Famous for its majestic waterfalls during the Khareef season, lush green forests, natural fresh springs, and a beautiful central lake where visitors can enjoy relaxing boat rides in nature.

👉 Book this destination in one click

Al Mughsail Beach (شاطئ المغسيل)

Al Mughsail is a stunning golden beach famous for its high limestone cliffs and natural sea blowholes, where sea water blasts high into the air. It offers panoramic views of the roaring waves of the Arabian Sea and is a favorite spot for visitors.

Marneef Cave (كهف المرنيف)

Right next to Mughsail Beach lies Marneef Cave, featuring beautiful natural rock formations, deep shaded benches, and fascinating viewpoints looking directly over the active shore blowholes and open cliffs of Salalah.
